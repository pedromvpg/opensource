# Open Source Hub Copy Improvements

Bitcoin Conference 2025 - Webflow Copy Document

---

## Tone Guidelines Applied

- **Technical & Credible**: Precise terminology, no marketing fluff
- **Cypherpunk Ethos**: Sovereignty, peer-to-peer, permissionless values
- **Bitcoin-first**: Protocol concepts (Lightning, Nostr, sats)
- **Direct**: Active voice, clear value statements

---

## 1. Hero Section

### Current
> **Headline**: "Builders Keep Building"
> **Subtitle**: Functional but not compelling (generic conference language)
> **CTA**: Free pass signup

### Proposed
**Headline**: "Still Building"
**Subtitle**: "Bitcoin, Lightning, Nostr, ecash, AI agents—two days with the devs shipping the future. Free passes for open source contributors."
**CTA**: "Claim Your Free Pass"

### Rationale
- "Still Building"—understated, implies persistence through market cycles and noise
- Lead with Bitcoin, then the stack: Lightning, Nostr, ecash, AI agents
- "Shipping the future" keeps it direct without categorizing the tech
- Added free pass mention directly in subtitle to reinforce the value prop

---

## 2. The Stage (Technical Panels)

### Current
> "Join technical heavy weights for panels and discussions on the state of Bitcoin development."

### Proposed
"Protocol engineers and core contributors present deep dives on protocol security, attacks and defenses, privacy improvements, and payments. Ossification vs. evolution. No marketing talks—just shipping code."

### Rationale
- "Technical heavy weights" is informal and vague
- Replaced with specific roles (protocol engineers, core contributors)
- Named actual technical topics (protocol security, attacks/defenses, privacy, payments)
- "Ossification vs. evolution" references the ongoing debate about Bitcoin's protocol development future—signals awareness of real developer discourse
- "No marketing talks" directly addresses developer skepticism of conferences
- "Shipping code" is developer vernacular

## 3. BitDevs Sessions

### Current
> "You can be well versed with or new to Bitcoin development—all are welcome to participate in these socratic-style discussions."

### Proposed
"Socratic seminars on Bitcoin protocol development. Review recent PRs, discuss BIPs, and debate implementation trade-offs. Participation expected—bring questions, not just ears."

### Rationale
- Original phrasing is grammatically awkward
- Removed hedging language ("all are welcome")
- Added specific activities (PR review, BIP discussion, implementation trade-offs)
- "Participation expected" sets clear expectations for the format
- "Bring questions, not just ears" encourages active engagement in authentic BitDevs style

---

## 4. 101 Stage (Bitcoin Basics)

### Current
> Too casual; doesn't convey the value proposition for beginners.

### Proposed
**Headline**: "101 Stage"
**Body**: "Foundational sessions on Bitcoin's monetary properties, self-custody, and running your own node. From first principles to first sats—no prior knowledge required."

### Rationale
- Specifies what "basics" actually means (monetary properties, self-custody, nodes)
- "First principles to first sats" creates a clear learning journey
- "No prior knowledge required" is more direct than "all are welcome"
- Maintains technical credibility while being accessible

---

## 5. Zap Speakers Live

### Current
> "Earn your space among the value for value bitcoin universe."

### Proposed
**Headline**: "Zap Speakers Live"
**Body**: "Speakers earn sats in real-time via Lightning zaps. Audience votes with value—not applause. Trust is measured in sats."

### Rationale
- Original was unclear about what "earn your space" means mechanically
- New copy explains the actual mechanism (Lightning zaps, real-time payments)
- "Audience votes with value—not applause" captures the V4V concept clearly
- "Trust is measured in sats" frames zaps as audience trust signal, not competition
- Technical specificity (Lightning, zaps, sats) appeals to target audience

---

## 6. Chain Duel Tournament

### Current
> "World renown Lightning game tournament" (typo: "renown" should be "renowned")
> Confusing description of gameplay mechanics.

### Proposed
**Headline**: "Chain Duel Tournament"
**Body**: "1v1 Lightning game tournament. Compete for a sats prize pool in the arcade-style Bitcoin game taking over conferences worldwide. Entry via Lightning—winners paid instantly."

### Rationale
- Fixed "world renown" typo
- "1v1" immediately communicates format
- "Sats prize pool" specifies the stakes
- "Arcade-style Bitcoin game" describes the experience
- "Entry via Lightning—winners paid instantly" demonstrates Bitcoin utility in action
- Emphasizes the global reach without awkward phrasing
