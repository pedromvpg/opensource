# Community Partner Recognition — Open Source Hub, Bitcoin Asia 2026

**What this is:** the concrete deliverable behind the outreach promise ("we credit your community on-site and cross-promote you"). It defines what a *Community Partner* is, what they give, what they get, and how each item actually gets executed in a ~7-week window at low cost.

**Framing:** This is a **partnership, not a sponsorship**. Partners don't pay — they bring their builders and co-promote. Recognition is *earned*, and it's designed to make each community feel like a co-owner of the Hub, not a logo on a slide.

---

## The deal (one-liner for partners)

> Bring your builders to the Open Source Hub and help spread the word — and we'll credit your community on-site, put your delegates in the room together, and cross-promote you to everyone who shows up.

**Partner gives:**
- Distributes a block of comp codes to their members (they pick who).
- One announcement to their community (Telegram / X / email / IG — their channel).
- A point person we can coordinate with.

**Partner gets:** everything in the tiers below.

---

## Two tiers (kept deliberately light)

| | **Community Partner** (default) | **Anchor Community Partner** (brings a real delegation / co-hosts) |
|---|---|---|
| Qualifies | Any partnered community distributing codes | ~15+ verified builders attending, **or** co-hosts a meetup/session |
| Partner wall (on-site) | ✅ listed | ✅ featured (larger / top) |
| Program-page listing | ✅ | ✅ + short blurb + link |
| Delegate badge ribbon | ✅ | ✅ |
| Social thank-you graphic | ✅ (group post) | ✅ (dedicated post) |
| Community meetup board | ✅ can pin their events | ✅ |
| Stage shout-out (MC) | — | ✅ named from the Open Source Stage |
| Nostr Stream mention / mini-interview | — | ✅ (schedule-permitting) |
| Self-hosted community table / meetup slot | — | ✅ (space-permitting) |
| Featured in post-event recap | — | ✅ |

Keep the bar for Anchor honest and low-friction — the point is to reward the communities who actually move people, not to gate recognition.

## The recognition menu — and how each gets done in 7 weeks

### On-site (physical)
1. **Community Partners wall** — one printed panel (or a looping screen) at the Hub entrance listing every partner community by name/logo. *Execution: one design file, printed once. Cheapest highest-visibility item. Confirm wall space with the Hub build-out.*
2. **Delegate badge ribbons** — a stick-on ribbon or badge sticker reading "[Community] delegate" (e.g. "Philippine Bitcoiners," "TBA," "Diamond Hands"). Members who came via a partner wear it. *Execution: ribbons are cheap and fast; ties into the existing badge/door flow. Makes each community physically visible and builds belonging — the single best "these are my people" signal.*
3. **Community meetup board** — a physical pin-board where partners post their own socials/meetups happening during conference week. *Execution: one board + sticky cards. Zero design cost; turns the Hub into the week's community hub.*
4. **Self-hosted community table / meetup slot** *(Anchor)* — a time window + a corner where a partner gathers their people. *Execution: depends on floor space and schedule — confirm early what's available before promising specific slots.*

### On-stage & broadcast (ties into existing Hub programming)
5. **MC stage shout-outs** *(Anchor)* — the Open Source Stage MC names partner communities at session starts / breaks. *Execution: a shout-out script; near-zero cost.*
6. **Nostr Stream recognition** *(Anchor)* — a "thank you to our community partners" segment, or a 3–5 min partner mini-interview, on the on-site Nostr broadcast (ReverendHodl). *Execution: schedule-permitting; fits the V4V / open-protocol ethos perfectly.*

### Digital & social (pre + post)
7. **Program-page listing** — a "Community Partners" section on the Hub page (2026.b.tc/opensource), each linked to the partner's site/socials. *Execution: one web update; recurring value (SEO + credibility). This is also proof for the partner that the deal is real when you first reach out.*
8. **Social thank-you graphic** — a co-brandable "Proud community partner of the Open Source Hub" graphic partners can reshare (gives *them* content, not just us). *Execution: one template, fill in each name. Drives their announcement.*
9. **Post-event recap feature** *(Anchor)* — named in the Hub recap / Nostr write-up. *Execution: part of normal post-event content.*

## What to lock before promising (reality check)

- **Wall space & signage** — confirm with the Hub build-out team what physical surfaces exist before committing to a printed partner wall.
- **Badge/ribbon logistics** — confirm ribbons can be added to the existing badge pipeline and who prints them.
- **Stage & Nostr Stream time** — MC shout-outs are easy; interview slots are schedule-dependent — mark them "schedule-permitting" in partner comms so you never over-promise.
- **Program-page edit** — get the "Community Partners" section live early; it's the proof point that makes the outreach credible.

## Sequencing (7-week sprint)
1. **Week 1:** stand up the program-page "Community Partners" section (even empty) + the social thank-you template. These make outreach credible.
2. **Weeks 1–4:** run outreach (the six templates); confirm partners + delegation sizes; add each to the page as they commit.
3. **Weeks 4–6:** finalize the partner wall design + ribbon list; confirm any Anchor stage/meetup slots.
4. **Conference week:** print wall + ribbons, brief the MC, schedule Nostr shout-outs, put up the meetup board.

> As a Community Partner of the Open Source Hub, [community] gets: your name on the Hub's Community Partners wall and program page, "[community] delegate" ribbons for your members' badges, a spot on the community meetup board, and a co-brandable partner graphic to share. Bring a crew and you're an Anchor Partner — a shout-out from the Open Source Stage, a mention on our live Nostr broadcast, and space to gather your people on the floor.
