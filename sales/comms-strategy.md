# Open Source Hub — Communications Strategy

**Goal:** Leverage the **Open Source Hub** brand to rally developers across four audiences to Bitcoin Asia 2026 (Hong Kong, late Aug), leading with *open source* for non-Bitcoin audiences and Bitcoin for Bitcoin-native ones — sounding like peers, not a marketing team.

---

## 1. Positioning — what we can honestly claim

- **"Open source" is the universal banner.** It's the shared value (FOSS, permissionless, build-in-public, sovereignty) that lets non-Bitcoin developers feel the Hub is for them.
- **AI is a real but narrow vertical** — decentralized / private / agentic AI, agents that pay & hold value, self-hostable inference, AI-×-Nostr. The Hub programs this (Maple, PPQ, nostr-mcp/lightning-mcp). It is **not** a general AI conference; a generic ML dev has no reason to come.
- **VR is a spectacle, not an audience.** The on-floor VR activation is an *experience to show*, not a dev vertical to recruit. It drives attention, assets, and FOMO across every track. Do **not** invite "VR developers" — there's no such scene here.
- **No bait-and-switch.** Lead non-Bitcoin audiences with open source + the honest AI angle; use VR as the thing we show. Never imply it's not a Bitcoin event — imply that open source is the reason to come regardless of stack.

---

## 2. Four audience tracks

| Track | Lead framing / vernacular | Hero value-give (the first line) | Bitcoin emphasis |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1 · Bitcoin-native** | Protocol, ship code, PRs, sats, sovereignty | Help their builders attend, connect, and participate | **High** — Bitcoin-centric |
| **2 · AI × open-source** | Agents, inference, open weights, self-host; Bitcoin as the rails | Give relevant projects and communities a useful place in the room | **Low** — a detail, not the headline |
| **3 · Students** | Open source + learning; jargon-light | Help members access workshops, maintainers, and practical learning | **Minimal** — just the event name |
| **4 · Pro OSS communities** | Open-source ethos, FOSS, maintainers, upstream | **Support what they already want to do** — access, visibility, participation | **Minimal** — hosted-at, not about |

VR spectacle cuts across all four (mostly in broadcast assets).

---

## 3. Value-give principle

Lead with **one relevant reason**, then open a discovery conversation. We know the tools available; we do not yet know which outcome matters to them.

**Categories we can draw from after discovery:**
- **Access:** organizer/influencer passes and complimentary community passes, with room to expand when demand is credible.
- **Visibility:** featured Partner placement on the Open Source page and partner amplification.
- **Participation:** ways to showcase work, gather their community, or contribute to the Hub.
- **Programming:** possible Open Source Stage consideration for a strong topic and audience fit. This is program-dependent, never guaranteed.

The first message should not prescribe a bundle. Signal that we can help with access, visibility, and participation, then ask what would make the partnership useful for their people. Once they answer, build a concrete offer around that need.

The exchange should remain proportional and light. Do not manufacture urgency, hide that Bitcoin Asia is the host event, or imply stage access is pay-to-play.

---

## 4. Voice — natural & concise

- **Peer, not promoter.** Real name/role (Pedro), plain language, active voice, 3–5 sentences.
- **Specific beats hype.** Name real people/projects/sessions. Kill superlatives ("world-class," "amazing," "don't miss") — devs read adjectives as spam and specifics as credibility.
- **One give, one ask.** Value first, single clear CTA.
- **Local vernacular per track** (see table). De-Bitcoin tracks 2–4 by making *open source* the noun and Bitcoin a detail.
- **Last mile is theirs.** For the member-facing reshare, hand the organizer an *adaptable draft* they rewrite in their own voice — their members trust their voice, not ours.

> I'd like to find a useful way to bring [community] into the Hub. We can help with access for your builders, visibility for the community, and ways to participate. Which would matter most to your community? Open to a quick conversation?

## 6. Organizer adaptable draft (member-facing reshare — they rewrite in their own voice)

> [community] is a Partner of the **Open Source Hub** at Bitcoin Asia (Hong Kong, late Aug). We have **[N] complimentary GA passes** for members — two days of open-source workshops, live project demos, and builders from across the region. If you can realistically make it to HK, claim one here: [CLAIM LINK] (code [CODE]).

Give organizers: this draft + both social assets + the pass code. Tell them to make it sound like *them*.

---

## 7. Asset strategy — two-tier

- **Broadcast (our socials + partner reshare):** a *small* set of **animated hero pieces** — motion earns its keep here. VR is the hero spectacle. See `motion-brief.md`.
- **1:1 outreach (DMs/emails):** **text-first, lean, at most one restrained static image** — a polished graphic reads as a mass campaign and lowers trust.
- **On-site + partner fill-in:** the static SVG templates (`assets/graphics/`).

**What performs:** short silent loops (4–6s), strong first frame, restrained-technical motion (glow + type-on, no flashy transitions), real footage/faces > renders. On X native video; Nostr image+text; Telegram/IG video/GIF.

## 9. Sequencing (~7-week sprint)

1. Program-page "Community Partners" section live (proof point) + static assets ready.
2. 1:1 outreach by track (text-first) → confirm partners + delegation sizes.
3. Motion-design handoff → animated heroes for broadcast + partner reshare.
4. During event: capture real VR + Hub footage → replaces teaser assets for post-event.
